Autex reimburses reasonable, work-related expenses so that cost doesn't get in the way of doing your job well. The guiding principle is simple: "reasonable" means what a thoughtful person would spend if it were their own money. When you're unsure whether an expense qualifies, apply that test — and if you're still unsure, ask your manager before you spend.

## What's covered

| Category | What's reimbursable | What's not |
|---|---|---|
| **Travel / business trips** | Flights, trains, and approved hotels for work travel | First-class upgrades (unless pre-approved), personal side trips added to business travel |
| **Meals on travel days** | Up to $XX per day while traveling for work | Alcohol (except at approved team events), meals for non-employees without prior approval |
| **Home office** | See the [Home Office Setup policy](/policies/remote-work/home-office-setup) for covered items within stipend | Furniture or equipment beyond the stipend without separate approval |
| **Software & tools** | Role-specific tools with manager approval | Duplicates of tools already available through the company |
| **Team events** | Team lunches, dinners, or offsites with prior manager approval and a defined budget | Events organized without a pre-approved budget |
| **Learning & development** | Courses, books, and conferences within your annual L&D budget | Expenses beyond the annual cap without explicit VP approval |

<Info>
  Dollar caps (marked as $XX above) are published in the HR portal and reviewed annually. Check there for the current figures before you travel or spend.
</Info>

## How to submit an expense report

<Steps>
  <Step title="Log in to the expense tool">
    Open the expense tool using the link in the HR portal. Sign in with your company email and SSO credentials. If you don't have access, contact IT.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Create a new report and attach receipts">
    Click **New Report** and give it a descriptive name (e.g., "NYC client visit — March 2025"). Add each expense as a line item and attach a receipt for every item over $XX. Missing receipts are the most common reason reimbursements are delayed or declined, so attach them at the time you create each line — don't wait until submission.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Categorize and justify each line item">
    Select the correct expense category from the dropdown for each line. In the business justification field, briefly explain the business purpose — "client dinner with Acme team ahead of renewal" is sufficient; "dinner" is not. Clear justifications speed up approval and protect you if Finance has questions later.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Submit for manager approval">
    Once your report is complete, click **Submit**. Your manager will receive an approval request by email. If your manager hasn't acted within three business days, follow up with them directly.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Warning>
  Submit all expense reports within **30 days** of the expense date. Late submissions may be declined, and Finance is not obligated to reimburse expenses reported more than 60 days after they were incurred.
</Warning>

## Approval and reimbursement timeline

Once you submit your report, here's what happens:

1. **Manager approval** — your manager reviews and approves (or returns for clarification).
2. **Finance review** — Finance completes their review within **3 business days** of manager approval.
3. **Reimbursement** — you'll receive payment either via payroll on your next pay cycle, or via ACH direct deposit within **5–7 business days**, depending on the timing and your payment setup.

If your reimbursement hasn't arrived within 10 business days of Finance approval, contact the Finance team directly.

## Corporate card

Some roles have access to a company corporate card for recurring or high-volume business expenses. Having a corporate card does not mean your expenses are automatically approved. You are still required to submit receipts and business justifications for every charge through the expense tool. Unjustified card charges will be flagged by Finance and may need to be repaid.

To find out whether your role qualifies for a corporate card, contact your manager or HR.

## Travel bookings

Always use the approved travel booking tool (linked in the HR portal) for flights and hotels. This ensures you're covered by the company's travel insurance, that invoices are centralized, and that your bookings comply with our [Travel Policy](/policies/travel-policy).

If you book outside the approved tool without prior written approval from your manager, your reimbursement may be capped at what the approved tool would have charged for an equivalent booking — or declined entirely.
