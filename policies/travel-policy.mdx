When your work requires you to travel, Autex covers the cost of reasonable, pre-approved business travel. All business travel must be booked through the approved travel booking tool — this ensures you're covered by the company's duty-of-care programme, that invoices are centralised, and that your spend stays within policy. You can find a link to the travel tool in the HR portal.

## Before you book

Every business trip requires approval before you book — not after. The approval level depends on the trip cost:

- **All trips**: get your manager's approval first.
- **Trips over $XXX in total estimated cost**: director-level approval is required in addition to manager approval.

Book through the travel tool using your company email address. If you book through a personal account or an external platform without prior written approval, your trip will not be covered by the company's travel insurance, and reimbursement may be partial or declined entirely.

## Booking guidelines

### Flights

| Situation | What's permitted |
|---|---|
| Flights under 5 hours | Economy class only |
| Flights over 8 hours | Business class permitted with manager approval |
| Flights between 6–8 hours with back-to-back meetings on arrival | Business class permitted with manager approval |
| Booking timing | Book at least **14 days in advance** wherever possible |

Booking early keeps costs down and protects the company. If you need to book within 14 days due to circumstances outside your control, note the reason in the travel tool.

### Hotels

Use the preferred hotel list in the travel tool where available. Rate caps apply:

- **Standard cities**: up to $XXX per night
- **High-cost cities** (San Francisco, New York City, London, and others listed in the travel tool): up to $XXX per night

If the preferred hotel is unavailable or the rate cap cannot be met in a given city, request an exception from your manager before booking. Document the exception in the travel tool.

### Ground transport

Use rideshare (Uber, Lyft, or local equivalent) or public transit for getting around. Rental cars are approved when:

- Your trip involves multiple stops that aren't practical by rideshare or transit, or
- Public transit or rideshare isn't reliably available at your destination.

When you rent, book a mid-size vehicle or smaller. Larger classes require manager approval.

## On the road

Keep receipts for every reimbursable expense. Submit your expense report within 30 days of returning — see the [Expense Reimbursement policy](/policies/expense-reimbursement) for full submission instructions.

**Meals** are covered up to **$XX per day** on travel days. You don't need itemized receipts for individual meals under the daily cap, but you do need a receipt or credit card record showing the total.

**Team dinners** during a trip can exceed the daily meal cap with prior manager approval. Get approval before the dinner, not after.

## Safety and emergencies

Your safety is the company's first priority when you travel. Follow these steps:

1. **Register your trip** in the travel tool before you depart. This activates the company's duty-of-care coverage and ensures someone knows where you are.
2. **Check travel advisories** before booking. If your destination has a government travel advisory at Level 2 or above, contact HR before booking. Trips to Level 3 or 4 destinations require explicit senior leadership approval.
3. **In an emergency abroad**: contact the travel insurer first (the emergency contact number is in the travel tool under "My Trips"). Then notify your manager and HR as soon as you're safe. Don't wait until you're back home.

<Warning>
  If you travel to a restricted country without prior approval, Autex cannot guarantee that travel insurance will cover incidents that occur during the trip. Always check with HR before booking travel to destinations with active advisories.
</Warning>

## Cancellations and changes

Cancel or change bookings as early as possible through the travel tool to minimize cancellation fees.

**Non-refundable bookings** require manager approval *before* you book them — not as a way to recover the cost after the fact if a trip is cancelled. If you book a non-refundable fare without approval and the trip is subsequently cancelled, the company may not reimburse the unused cost.

If a trip is cancelled due to a company decision (reorganization, project cancellation, etc.), Finance will absorb any non-refundable costs regardless of pre-approval status. Contact your manager to initiate that process.
