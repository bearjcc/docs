The standard at Autex is a simple one: treat everyone with respect, act with integrity, and take responsibility for your actions. This code applies everywhere you represent Autex — in the office, on video calls, in Slack, at company events, and in any external communication made on the company's behalf.

## Our expectations

### Respectful communication

Debate and disagreement are healthy — we encourage you to challenge ideas and push back on proposals. What we don't accept is personal attacks. Disagree with ideas, not people. This applies equally in Slack threads, code reviews, one-on-ones, and large all-hands meetings. Sarcasm, condescension, and dismissiveness all erode trust even when no single comment feels like a big deal, so hold yourself to a high standard consistently.

### Inclusion

Autex is committed to an environment where everyone can do their best work. We do not tolerate discrimination or harassment on the basis of race, gender, gender identity or expression, sexual orientation, religion, disability, national origin, age, or any other characteristic protected by law. This includes exclusionary jokes, slurs, and "just kidding" comments — if it demeans or excludes a group of people, it has no place here.

### Integrity

Be honest with your colleagues, your manager, and your customers. If you make a mistake — in your work, in a customer interaction, or anywhere else — own it, fix it where you can, and be transparent about what happened. Covering up errors, misrepresenting facts, or taking credit for others' work are all violations of this principle.

### Confidentiality

You will have access to information that is sensitive — about the company's strategy, finances, customers, and your colleagues. Respect that confidentiality. Don't share internal information outside the company without authorization, and handle customer data according to our data-handling guidelines. If you're unsure whether something is confidential, assume it is until you check.

### Conflicts of interest

Your professional judgment should serve Autex and its customers, not your personal interests. If you find yourself in a situation where a personal interest — financial, relational, or otherwise — could influence a business decision, disclose it to your manager or HR before acting. When in doubt, ask. Disclosing a potential conflict is never the wrong move.

---

## What counts as harassment

Harassment includes, but is not limited to:

- Unwelcome comments about a person's appearance, identity, or personal life
- Persistent unwanted contact after someone has asked you to stop
- Sharing private information about a colleague without their consent
- Creating, forwarding, or displaying material that demeans or sexualizes colleagues

If you're unsure whether a behavior crosses the line, ask yourself how you'd feel if it were directed at you and witnessed by everyone at the company. When in doubt, don't do it.

## Reporting a concern

You should never feel like you have to tolerate behavior that makes you uncomfortable or unsafe at work. Here's how to report it:

<Steps>
  <Step title="Address it directly (if you feel safe doing so)">
    If you're comfortable, tell the person directly that their behavior is unwelcome and you'd like it to stop. Sometimes people are unaware of the impact of their actions, and a direct conversation resolves the issue. You are never obligated to do this if you don't feel safe.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Report to your manager or HR">
    If a direct conversation isn't possible, isn't safe, or doesn't resolve the issue, report the situation to your manager or the HR team. Provide as much detail as you can — what happened, when, and who was involved.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Use the anonymous hotline if your manager is involved">
    If the concern involves your manager, or if you prefer not to report by name, go directly to HR or use the anonymous reporting hotline. You can find the link in the HR portal. All reports are taken seriously, investigated promptly, and handled with as much discretion as possible.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Warning>
  Retaliation against anyone who makes a good-faith report — or who participates in an investigation — is itself a violation of this code of conduct and will be treated as seriously as the underlying complaint.
</Warning>

## Consequences

Violations of this code may result in a formal warning, a performance improvement plan, or termination of employment, depending on the nature and severity of the behavior. Serious violations — including harassment, discrimination, or deliberate dishonesty — may result in immediate termination. Every situation is reviewed individually, and we are committed to a fair process.

If you have questions about this policy or want to talk through a situation before deciding whether to report it, HR is available for confidential conversations.
