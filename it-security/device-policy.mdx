Every device you use to access company systems — whether it was issued by the company or is your own personal device — must meet minimum security requirements. This policy applies to laptops, desktops, tablets, and mobile phones. If a device does not meet these requirements, IT may block its access to company resources until it is brought into compliance.

## Company-issued devices

Company-issued devices are automatically enrolled in mobile device management (MDM) when you set them up. MDM enforces the following controls on your behalf:

- **Full-disk encryption** — all data on the device is encrypted at rest
- **Screen lock** — the screen locks automatically after 5 minutes of inactivity
- **Approved OS versions** — your device will receive prompts to update when your OS version falls out of the approved range
- **Endpoint security agent** — antivirus and threat detection software runs continuously in the background
- **Remote wipe capability** — if the device is lost or stolen, IT can erase it remotely

<Warning>
  Do not remove MDM profiles from your device or disable any security software. Doing so may result in immediate loss of access to company systems and may trigger a disciplinary review. If you believe a security control is interfering with legitimate work, contact IT to discuss alternatives before making any changes.
</Warning>

## Personal devices (BYOD)

You may use a personal device to access company systems, provided it meets the following minimum requirements:

- **Screen lock** is enabled with a PIN, password, or biometric
- **Full-disk encryption** is turned on (this is the default on modern iOS and Android devices, and can be enabled in macOS and Windows settings)
- **Operating system** is kept up to date with current security patches
- **Sensitive company data** is not stored locally on the device — use approved cloud storage tools instead

Personal devices are not enrolled in MDM unless you opt in. In the event of a security incident involving your device, IT will only target the work profile or work-related data — your personal data is not accessed or wiped.

## Approved and prohibited uses

### Approved
- Performing work tasks and accessing company systems
- Using communication and collaboration tools (Slack, email, video calls)
- Reasonable personal use that does not interfere with device security

### Prohibited
- Installing software designed to circumvent security controls (VPN-bypass tools, jailbreaks, rooting utilities)
- Storing company data in personal cloud storage — use only approved tools such as Google Drive or the designated cloud storage platform
- Connecting a company-issued laptop to untrusted networks (public Wi-Fi, hotel networks) without using the company VPN
- Allowing family members or others to use your device for work tasks, even briefly

## Lost or stolen devices

<Warning>
  If your device is lost or stolen, report it to IT immediately — do not wait to see if it turns up. Contact IT via Slack **#it-help** or email the help desk directly. IT will remotely lock the device and, if necessary, initiate a remote wipe to protect company data. Acting quickly significantly reduces the risk of a data breach.
</Warning>

## End of employment

Return all company-issued devices on or before your last day of employment. IT will provide instructions for wiping personal data and completing the return process. Devices not returned on time may result in deductions per your employment agreement.
