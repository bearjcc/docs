Access to company systems follows the principle of least privilege — you receive access to what you need to do your job, and nothing more. This keeps sensitive data protected and limits the blast radius of any accidental or malicious activity. If your role changes, submit a new request rather than accumulating access over time.

## How to request access

<Steps>
  <Step title="Submit a ticket">
    Open a ticket in the IT portal or post in Slack **#it-requests**. Include the following information in your request:

    - **System or resource** you need access to
    - **Access level** required (see the table below if you're unsure)
    - **Business justification** — a brief explanation of why you need this access for your role

    The more detail you provide, the faster IT can fulfill your request.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Approval">
    Your manager receives an approval notification and must sign off before IT fulfills the request. Some access levels require additional sign-off from a director or the security team — refer to the access levels table below to see what applies to your request.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Fulfillment">
    Once approved, IT provisions your access. Standard tools are fulfilled within **1 business day**. Production systems and anything involving customer data are fulfilled within **3 business days** to allow time for additional security review.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Access levels

The table below describes the four access tiers, what they include, and who must approve each request.

| Level | Examples | Approval required |
|---|---|---|
| **Standard** | Slack channels, Notion, Figma | Manager |
| **Elevated** | AWS (non-production), staging environments | Manager + IT |
| **Privileged** | Production systems, customer data | Manager + Security |
| **Admin** | Identity provider, billing systems | Director + Security |

If you're unsure which level applies to your request, ask in **#it-requests** and IT will advise.

## Shared credentials and service accounts

Do not share your personal credentials with colleagues, even temporarily. If a team needs shared access to a system, request a dedicated service account through IT. Service accounts are audited quarterly to ensure they remain necessary and appropriately scoped.

## Access reviews

IT runs quarterly access reviews across the company. When a review is underway, you receive an email listing all the systems and permissions currently associated with your account. Review this list carefully and flag anything you no longer need — access that has gone unused is revoked after **30 days** of inactivity.

Keeping your access current protects both you and the company. If you no longer need something, say so.

## Offboarding

All access is revoked on your last day of employment. If you are a manager offboarding a team member, notify IT **at least two business days in advance** to allow time for any necessary handoffs — for example, transferring ownership of shared accounts or service credentials.

## Emergencies

<Warning>
  If you need emergency access outside of normal channels — for example, during an incident where a critical system is inaccessible — contact the IT help desk immediately via Slack **#it-help** or by phone. Include your manager on the request so IT can verify and prioritize appropriately. Do not attempt to work around access controls on your own.
</Warning>
