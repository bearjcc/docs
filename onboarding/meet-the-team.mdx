Knowing who does what — and who to go to when things get complicated — saves you time and frustration. This page gives you a map of Autex's leadership and team structure so you can orient yourself quickly, even before you've met everyone in person.

## Leadership

The table below lists the core leadership team and when it's appropriate to reach out to each person. For current names and Slack handles, check the **employee directory in the HR portal** or the **Slack directory** — both are kept up to date.

| Role | Slack | When to reach out |
|---|---|---|
| **Chief Executive Officer (CEO)** | See HR portal or Slack directory | For genuinely urgent, company-wide issues — DM directly only when it can't wait |
| **Chief Technology Officer (CTO)** | See HR portal or Slack directory | Engineering escalations that cannot be resolved at the team or VP level |
| **VP of People** | See HR portal or Slack directory | HR and benefits questions, policy concerns, and anything related to people operations |
| **VP of Engineering** | See HR portal or Slack directory | Decisions affecting the broader engineering organisation — team structure, tooling, standards |

<Note>
  For day-to-day questions, always start with your direct manager or your onboarding buddy before escalating to leadership. Most questions have a closer answer.
</Note>

## Teams

Autex is organised into four main teams. Each team has a distinct scope, but they work closely together — especially Engineering, Product, and Design, who are embedded in the same squads.

<CardGroup cols={2}>
  <Card title="Engineering" icon="code">
    Owns the product infrastructure, core services, and developer tooling. Engineering is broken into **squads organised by domain** (for example: platform, growth, data). Each squad owns a vertical slice of the product from infrastructure to user-facing features.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Product" icon="map">
    Works across Engineering and Design to define what gets built and why. Product is responsible for roadmap planning, prioritisation, and making sure the team is solving the right problems for users.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Design" icon="pen-nib">
    Covers product design, brand design, and content design. Designers are **embedded directly in squads** rather than working as a central pool, so you'll typically find your squad's designer at your squad's standups and planning sessions.
  </Card>
  <Card title="People & Operations" icon="building">
    Encompasses HR, recruiting, IT, finance, and office operations. If you have questions about your contract, benefits, equipment, or expenses, People & Ops is your starting point.
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

### Engineering

Engineering squads are each responsible for a specific domain of the product. Each squad typically includes engineers, a product manager, and an embedded designer. If you're joining Engineering, your manager will tell you which squad you're in and who your squadmates are during your first week.

<Tip>
  Ask your manager for a link to your squad's project board and Slack channel on day one — that's where most of the day-to-day work happens.
</Tip>

### Product

Product managers are aligned to squads, which means each PM owns the roadmap and prioritisation for a specific part of the product. If you need to understand why something is on the roadmap — or why something isn't — the aligned PM is the right person to ask.

### Design

Designers follow the squad model too. Your squad's designer attends your team rituals and is your primary design collaborator. For brand or company-wide design questions, reach out in the #design Slack channel.

### People & Operations

People & Ops handles everything from onboarding paperwork to IT tickets to office logistics. Key contacts within P&O include:

- **HR** — employment contracts, benefits enrolment, leave requests
- **IT / Help desk** — hardware, software access, security
- **Recruiting** — open roles and referrals
- **Finance** — expenses, invoices, budget questions

## Finding anyone at Autex

The **employee directory** in the HR portal is your fastest route to finding any colleague. Each profile shows:

- Full name and job title
- Team and squad
- Location and time zone
- Slack handle

If you're not sure who owns a specific system, process, or decision, post in **#ask-anything** on Slack. The channel is actively monitored and someone will point you in the right direction — no question is too basic.

<Tip>
  When you start a new project, spend five minutes in the directory identifying the PM, designer, and any cross-functional partners you'll need. Knowing who they are before you need them makes everything faster.
</Tip>
