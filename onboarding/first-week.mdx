Your first week is about building context — not proving yourself. The best thing you can do is ask a lot of questions, meet your teammates, and develop a clear picture of what the team is working on and why. By Friday you should have shipped at least one small thing (even a documentation fix or a merged PR counts) and feel confident about what you're stepping into.

## Goals for week one

By the end of your first week, aim to have achieved all of the following:

<CardGroup cols={2}>
  <Card title="All tools active" icon="circle-check">
    Every account is set up and every tool from the laptop setup guide is installed and working.
  </Card>
  <Card title="First 1:1 with your manager" icon="comments">
    A dedicated conversation to align on expectations, priorities, and how you'll work together.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Team introductions" icon="people-group">
    A brief intro with each member of your immediate team — you know their names, roles, and what they own.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Rough picture of priorities" icon="map">
    You can describe the team's current sprint or roadmap focus without needing to look it up.
  </Card>
  <Card title="A project for week two" icon="rocket">
    You and your manager have agreed on a concrete piece of work to start in week two.
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

## Recommended daily schedule

Use this as a guide, not a strict timetable. Your manager may adjust the order based on what's happening that week.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Day 1 — Work through the first-day checklist">
    Spend most of today on access, setup, and introductions. Don't jump into deep technical work yet.

    <Card title="First Day Checklist" icon="list-check" href="/onboarding/first-day">
      Everything you need to complete on day one, in order.
    </Card>
  </Step>

  <Step title="Days 2–3 — Team introductions">
    Book **20-minute intro slots** with each of your direct teammates. Keep it informal — introduce yourself, ask what they're working on, and ask what you should know about how the team operates.

    A few questions that tend to work well:
    - What are you focused on right now?
    - What's the biggest challenge the team is working through?
    - Is there anything you wish you'd known in your first week?

    <Tip>
      Send a short Slack message before booking the calendar invite. It feels less cold and most people respond faster.
    </Tip>
  </Step>

  <Step title="Day 4 — First 1:1 with your manager">
    This is your first formal check-in. Come prepared with:

    - A list of any access or tooling issues still unresolved
    - Questions about the team's priorities and how your role fits in
    - Any confusion about processes or expectations

    Your manager will likely walk you through the next 30-day plan and confirm what project you'll pick up in week two.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Day 5 — Reflect and write down what you learned">
    Block an hour on Friday afternoon to consolidate what you've absorbed this week. Write down:

    - The names and roles of everyone you met
    - The team's current priorities as you understand them
    - Three questions you still want answered

    <Note>
      This exercise is for you, not for your manager. The act of writing forces gaps in your understanding to the surface, and it gives you a useful reference point when week two gets busy.
    </Note>
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Things worth reading this week

Don't try to read everything. Focus on these three areas and go deeper once you have more context.

| Resource | What it covers | Link |
|---|---|---|
| **Meet the team** | Org chart, team structure, and how to find the right person | [Meet the Team](/onboarding/meet-the-team) |
| **Company handbook** | Policies, values, benefits, and how Autex operates | [Company Handbook](/policies/index) |
| **Your department's section** | Processes, standards, and context specific to your team | Find it in the KB sidebar |

<Tip>
  When you read the handbook, flag anything that surprises you or contradicts what someone told you verbally. Those gaps are worth raising with your manager or HR.
</Tip>
