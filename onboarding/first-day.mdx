Your first day is about getting access, getting oriented, and meeting the people who will help you hit the ground running. Don't worry about learning everything at once — focus on the checklist below, and flag anything that isn't working to your manager or the IT help desk right away.

## Before you start

Before your first day, your manager will send a welcome email to your personal address containing:

- A link to activate your **company email account**
- Your **start date and schedule** (hours, location or remote setup, first-day agenda)
- An invite to the company **Slack workspace**

<Note>
  If any of these items are missing 24 hours before your start date, reach out to your manager directly or email HR at **people@autex.com** so they can sort it out before day one.
</Note>

---

## First-day checklist

Work through the steps below in order — each one unlocks the next.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Activate your company email">
    Open the activation link in your welcome email and follow the prompts to create your account. Choose a strong, unique password and **enable two-factor authentication (2FA) immediately** — you will not be able to access most internal systems without it.

    <Tip>
      Use an authenticator app (such as Google Authenticator or Authy) rather than SMS for stronger security.
    </Tip>
  </Step>

  <Step title="Set up your laptop">
    Follow the laptop setup guide to install required software, configure security settings, and register your device with IT.

    <Card title="Laptop Setup Guide" icon="laptop" href="/onboarding/tools/laptop-setup">
      Step-by-step instructions for configuring your Autex-issued machine.
    </Card>
  </Step>

  <Step title="Connect to the VPN">
    The VPN is required before you can reach any internal systems — dashboards, the HR portal, internal documentation, and more. Get this working before you try anything else.

    <Card title="VPN Setup Guide" icon="shield-halved" href="/onboarding/tools/vpn-setup">
      Instructions for installing and connecting to the Autex VPN.
    </Card>
  </Step>

  <Step title="Claim your accounts">
    You need active accounts in three places:

    | Tool | Purpose |
    |---|---|
    | **Slack** | Team communication |
    | **Project tracker** | Tasks and sprint boards |
    | **HR portal** | Payroll, benefits, time off |

    Credentials are either included in your welcome email or in the IT onboarding ticket assigned to you. If you cannot find them, open a ticket with the IT help desk.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Join your Slack channels">
    Ask your manager which channels are relevant for your role. At a minimum, join these three:

    - **Your team channel** — day-to-day team conversation (your manager will give you the exact name)
    - **#ask-anything** — the right place to ask any question, no matter how basic
    - **#announcements** — company-wide updates you should not miss

    <Tip>
      Search Slack for channels that match your department or project area — joining a few extras on day one gives you useful context.
    </Tip>
  </Step>

  <Step title="Read today's standup notes">
    Before your first meeting, find the standup notes for your team in the project tracker or your team Slack channel. Reading what the team is actively working on gives you enough context to follow along without needing everything explained from scratch.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Meet your onboarding buddy">
    HR pairs every new hire with an onboarding buddy — a colleague outside your direct team who can answer day-to-day questions without any awkwardness. Send them a message on Slack and book a **30-minute intro call** for today or tomorrow.

    <Note>
      Your buddy's name and Slack handle are included in your welcome email. If they aren't, ask HR.
    </Note>
  </Step>
</Steps>

## People to meet today

You do not need to schedule formal meetings with everyone, but make sure you connect with these three people on day one.

| Person | What to cover |
|---|---|
| **Your manager** | Walk through your first 30 days — goals, expectations, how you'll work together, and how to escalate blockers. |
| **Your onboarding buddy** | Day-to-day questions, team culture, unwritten norms, and anything you feel awkward asking in a group. |
| **IT help desk** | Confirm your laptop, VPN, and account access are all set up correctly. Raise any issues now rather than on day two. |

<Tip>
  At the end of day one, jot down two or three things you are still confused about. Bring those questions to your onboarding buddy or manager tomorrow — it's expected and appreciated.
</Tip>
