Taking time away from work is an important part of staying healthy, creative, and effective. Autex operates on an **unlimited PTO policy** for salaried employees — there is no fixed accrual, no balance to track, and no cap on days taken. The policy is trust-based: you and your manager work together to make sure your time away doesn't leave the team in a difficult position. In practice, most employees take **three to five weeks per year** plus company holidays.

## Leave types

The table below summarizes every type of leave available to you and how each one works.

| Leave type | Accrual / limit | Approval required? | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| Vacation / personal | No fixed accrual | Yes (manager) | Request via the process below |
| Sick leave | No fixed limit | No | Notify your manager and mark it in the HR portal |
| Company holidays | ~10 federal holidays / year | N/A | Full calendar published in the HR portal each January |
| Parental leave | See [Benefits](/hr-people/benefits) | Yes (HR) | XX weeks paid primary, XX weeks secondary |
| Bereavement | Up to 5 days paid | No | Contact HR for extended leave beyond 5 days |
| Jury duty / civic leave | Fully paid, duration of service | No | Send documentation to HR after your service |

<Info>
  The company holiday calendar is updated every January and is available in the HR portal. Check it at the start of the year to plan ahead.
</Info>

## How to request time off

<Steps>
  <Step title="Check in with your manager first">
    Before submitting anything formally, have a quick conversation with your manager — especially if your absence falls near a product launch, an on-call rotation, or another time-sensitive period. Getting early alignment avoids surprises on both sides.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Submit your request in the HR portal">
    For any absence **longer than two days**, submit a time-off request in the HR portal **at least two weeks in advance**. Your manager will receive a notification and can approve or flag a conflict directly in the portal.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Block your calendar and set your out-of-office reply">
    Once your request is approved, block your calendar for the full duration and set an out-of-office auto-reply on your email. Include the date you'll be back and a contact who can help while you're away.
  </Step>
  <Step title="For sick days, submit in the portal same day or next business day">
    Sick days don't require advance approval. Notify your manager as early as you can (a Slack message is fine), then log the absence in the HR portal on the day you're out or on the next business day when you return.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Blackout periods

Some teams restrict time-off requests during critical periods — for example, the weeks immediately before a major product release or a high-stakes customer deadline. Your manager will communicate any blackout windows in advance.

<Warning>
  If you need to request time off during a declared blackout period, you'll need approval from both your **manager and their director**. Plan accordingly and start the conversation early.
</Warning>

## Carryover and payout

Because Autex's PTO policy is unlimited, there is **no carryover** from one year to the next and **no payout of unused time** upon termination. The expectation is that you take the time you need throughout the year rather than banking it.

If you have questions about any leave type or need to discuss an unusual situation, post in **#people-help** on Slack or reach out to the People team through the HR portal.
