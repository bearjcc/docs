Equity is a meaningful part of total compensation for most roles at Autex. It gives you a direct stake in the company's growth and aligns your long-term interests with the company's success. This page covers how different grant types work, how vesting is structured, and what to think about when exercising options or approaching a liquidity event. For amounts and specifics that are personal to you, review your grant agreement or contact HR.

## Grant types

Autex issues two types of equity, and your offer letter specifies which type you received.

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Stock Options (ISOs and NSOs)">
    A stock option gives you the **right to purchase shares** at a fixed price — the **strike price** — set at the time of your grant. Options have value when the company's fair market value (FMV) rises above your strike price. You don't own shares until you exercise the option and pay the strike price.

    - **ISOs (Incentive Stock Options)** are issued to employees and carry potential tax advantages under US law.
    - **NSOs (Non-Qualified Stock Options)** may be issued to employees, contractors, or advisors and are taxed differently at exercise.

    Your grant agreement specifies which type you hold.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="RSUs (Restricted Stock Units)">
    An RSU is a **promise of shares** delivered to you on a vesting date. Unlike options, RSUs have value as long as the company has value — there's no strike price to overcome. This makes them lower risk than options, though they also typically reflect a different expectation of company stage and value.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

<Warning>
  The tax treatment of ISOs, NSOs, and RSUs differs significantly. Do not assume the same rules apply to each type. Review your grant agreement and consult a qualified tax advisor before making any decisions about exercising or selling.
</Warning>

## Vesting schedule

Equity doesn't all become yours on day one — it vests over time as you continue working at Autex. The standard schedule is **four years with a one-year cliff**.

Here's how it works:

<Steps>
  <Step title="The cliff (year 1)">
    No equity vests until your **one-year anniversary**. If you leave before that date, you receive no equity from that grant.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Cliff vesting (25%)">
    On your one-year anniversary, **25% of your total grant** vests all at once.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Monthly vesting (remaining 75%)">
    After the cliff, the remaining 75% vests in **equal monthly installments over the following 36 months**, until your four-year anniversary.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Refresher grants">
    If you receive a refresher (top-up) grant, it typically vests **monthly from the grant date without a cliff**. Your grant agreement will confirm the terms.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Exercising options

You can exercise vested stock options at any time while you are employed at Autex. To do so, contact the equity plan administrator — instructions are in your equity portal.

### After leaving Autex

<Warning>
  If you leave Autex, you typically have **90 days from your last day** to exercise any vested options before they expire. After that window closes, unexercised options are forfeited. Check your specific grant agreement, as windows can vary.
</Warning>

This 90-day window is short. If you're considering leaving and hold vested options, think through your exercise decision **before** your last day — not after.

### Tax considerations

The tax consequences of exercising options depend on whether you hold ISOs or NSOs, how long you hold the shares after exercising, and the difference between your strike price and the current FMV.

<Warning>
  Consult a qualified tax advisor before exercising options. The difference between ISO and NSO treatment — particularly around the Alternative Minimum Tax (AMT) for ISOs — can be significant, and timing decisions can affect your tax bill materially.
</Warning>

## Equity events

If Autex undergoes a **liquidity event** — such as an IPO or an acquisition — your vested equity becomes actionable:

- **IPO:** Vested shares become tradeable on the public market, typically after a lock-up period of around 180 days.
- **Acquisition:** Vested shares are typically converted to cash or acquiring-company stock, depending on the deal structure.

For **unvested shares**, some grant agreements include **acceleration clauses** that vest a portion (or all) of unvested equity upon an acquisition. These terms vary by grant.

<Warning>
  Do not assume your unvested shares will accelerate in an acquisition. Review your grant agreement carefully, and contact HR if you have questions about what your specific agreement says.
</Warning>

## Getting help

Equity can be complex, and you shouldn't have to navigate it alone. Here's where to go:

- **HR or equity plan administrator** — for questions about your grant, vesting status, or how to exercise.
- **Annual equity 101 session** — Autex runs an equity education session for new hires each year. Watch for the invite in your onboarding checklist or ask HR when the next session is scheduled.
- **Tax advisor** — for anything involving the financial or tax implications of your decisions. HR cannot provide tax advice.
