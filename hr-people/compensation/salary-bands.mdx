Salary bands tie each role level to a defined pay range, making compensation consistent and defensible across the company. Rather than negotiating from scratch for every hire or promotion, Autex uses bands to ensure that people doing equivalent work at equivalent levels are paid equitably. Bands are reviewed annually against external market data and adjusted when the market moves.

## How leveling works

Every role at Autex maps to a level — **L1 through L6**, also referred to as Associate, Mid, Senior, Staff, and Principal. Levels aren't just titles; they reflect the scope of your impact, the degree of autonomy you exercise, and the contributions the company expects from you at that stage of your career.

Your level is stated in your offer letter. If you're unsure of your current level, ask your manager — it should never be a mystery.

| Level | Title | Focus |
|---|---|---|
| L1 | Associate | Building foundational skills; works with close guidance |
| L2 | Mid | Independent contributor; owns well-scoped work |
| L3 | Senior | Leads complex work; growing cross-team influence |
| L4 | Staff | Drives multi-team or cross-functional initiatives |
| L5 | Principal | Company-wide technical or organizational impact |
| L6 | Distinguished | Rare, transformational scope of impact |

## How bands are structured

Each band has three reference points: a **minimum**, a **midpoint**, and a **maximum**.

- **Minimum** — the floor for someone entering the role or ramping up to full expectations.
- **Midpoint** — the target for a strong-fit candidate who is fully effective in the role. Offers are typically made at or near the midpoint.
- **Maximum** — the ceiling for someone who has been highly effective in the role over time.

Where you sit within your band reflects your tenure, performance, and readiness relative to the full scope of the level — not just time in seat.

## How your base salary changes

There are three paths through which your base salary can increase.

### 1. Annual review cycle

Merit increases are tied directly to your performance rating from the annual review. They are expressed as a percentage of your current base salary and typically fall in the **0–8% range**, with higher ratings corresponding to larger increases.

For more on how performance ratings are determined, see [Performance Reviews](/hr-people/performance-reviews).

### 2. Promotions

When you are promoted to a new level, your salary is raised to at least the **midpoint of the new band**. Promotions are discussed during the annual review cycle, though in exceptional cases they can happen off-cycle.

### 3. Market corrections

If an annual band review reveals that a role is paying below market — even for employees who are performing well — Autex may adjust salaries outside of the normal annual cycle. These corrections are not tied to performance; they reflect the market moving.

## Geographic adjustments

Autex sets pay based on the market for each role, adjusted for **cost of labor** in your region. This means remote employees in different geographies may sit in different bands for the same role.

<Note>
  If you relocate, your band may change. Notify HR before a move so they can confirm whether your compensation will be affected.
</Note>
